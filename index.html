Kishor Kumar

The Melodius Singer

Early Years

  • Born on August 4th, 1929 in Khandwa, Madhya Pradesh
  • Father Kunwar Kumar Ganguly was a lawyer and his mother Gouri Devi was a home-maker
  • Completed his schooling at the Christian Missionary School, Khandwa
  • Was a multi-talented child who showed great interest in music, singing, and acting at a very young age
  • Moved to Mumbai in 1945 to pursue his career in music and singing
  • Started his career with the Bombay Talkies studio as a chorus singer
  • Sang his first solo song ‘Marne ki duayen kyon mangu’ in the movie ‘Ziddi’ in 1948
  • Became one of the most successful playback singers in Bollywood in the 1950s and 1960s
  • Won several awards for his singing and acting, including the National Film Award for Best Male Playback Singer in 1974

Achievement

  • Kishor Kumar was a renowned Indian playback singer, actor, lyrics, and music composer.
  • He won 8 Filmfare Awards for Best Playback Singer and holds the record for the most number of Filmfare Awards in that category.
  • He was awarded the Padma Bhushan in 2003 and the Dadasaheb Phalke Award in 2011.
  • He sang in many Indian languages including Hindi, Bengali, Marathi, Assamese, Gujarati, Kannada, Bhojpuri, Malayalam, and Oriya.
  • He had a distinctive, melodious voice and became popular for his duets with Lata Mangeshkar.
  • He composed music for some of the all-time classic films such as Anand, Aradhana, and Amar Prem.
  • He also wrote lyrics and acted in films, including the cult classic comedy Chalti Ka Naam Gaadi.
